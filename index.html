Key insight: Missions produce CC, tournaments consume CC. When balanced, the economy is self-regulating - the operator controls the net flow. Wheel, Chests, and Packs sit at the intersection: they are both things players buy with CC and things they earn through progression.

04 - Phase 2

Ramp

Late May, ~2 weeks before WC. Introduce WC collectibles gradually. Build familiarity before the main event.

Week 1: Discovery

At Level 10, the WC Collection Tab appears with a banner: "World Cup Collection is coming!" Player gets 1 free starter pack. Opens it. Sees 16 countries, 3 fragments placed, everything else at 0%. The grid is mostly empty - but it's their grid now.

WC-themed missions start appearing: "Place 5 bets on international football → earn 1 pack." Players earn their first packs through normal betting. The loop clicks: bet → XP → level → pack → collect.

Week 2: Investment

WC Packs appear in the Shop - buyable with CC they've been accumulating. First WC tournaments launch: "Most WC bets this week → 3 Rare packs." Players are now spending CC they used to hoard.

By end of week 2, players have opened 5-8 packs. Maybe 1-2 Common countries are nearly complete. The collection grid is filling up. They check it daily. They compare progress with friends. "Invite friends, your crew leaderboard goes live" starts appearing.

The partial collection creates loss aversion. They've invested time and CC. They won't walk away now. This is exactly where you want them when WC kicks off.

06 - Phase 4

Chase

WC duration, ~5 weeks. The collection race is on. Every system is working together to sustain momentum.

The Collection Race

Week 1-2: Common Countries Fill Up

Players complete their first Common countries fast. Satisfaction hits early. The collection grid starts filling with color. The hook is set - they've invested, they won't stop now.

Week 2-3: Uncommon Chase Begins

Common countries done, attention shifts to Uncommon. Drop rates are lower, missions are harder, packs are more valuable. Bet frequency increases - players want more attempts.

Week 3-4: Rare Becomes the Goal

Dedicated players are deep into the collection. They're checking leaderboards, comparing with friends, spending CC on Rare packs in the shop. Cross-sell missions activate: "Play 20 casino rounds → earn 1 Rare pack."

Week 4-5: Legendary Hunt

The most engaged players are chasing Legendary fragments. Every pack opening is a micro-event. Every Legendary drop is a story. These players are betting at maximum volume and pulling friends in via crew leaderboards.

Cross-Sell: Sportsbook → Casino

"Play 20 slots rounds → earn 1 Rare pack"

Casino-themed missions that reward WC packs push sportsbook players to try casino. This is invisible revenue: a sportsbook bettor becomes a dual-channel player. They came for the collection, they stayed for the slots.

Progression Acceleration

As players complete items, drop probability redistributes to remaining items. Progress accelerates the more you collect. This prevents late-game frustration and rewards commitment. A player at 90% completion fills faster than a player at 50%.

Live Dashboard Control

All drop rates, fragment counts, mission targets, and reward values are adjustable from the dashboard mid-campaign. Real-time levers:

Engagement too low?

Boost drop rates. Add flash missions. Increase daily login rewards.

Rewards too generous?

Tighten drop probabilities. Reduce pack sizes. Adjust CC pricing.

Want a spike?

Flash tournament: "Next 2 hours - double pack rewards." Time-limited shop bundle.

07 - Phase 5

Legacy

Post-WC, ~3 weeks. Graceful de-escalation back to regular progression. WC collectibles become permanent trophies. Levels and sublevels continue - only the event-themed rewards change.

Week 1: Final Whistle

Collection stays open 7 more days. "Last chance" urgency drives final push. WC tournaments and missions begin winding down. Login rewards start transitioning to regular progression.

Week 2: Trophy Case

Collection freezes into permanent trophy case. Permanent badges for completers. Time-limited XP boost bonus (configurable duration from dashboard). Exclusive shop tier.

Week 3: Normalize

Event rewards fully transition to regular progression rewards. Levels and sublevels continue unchanged - only the WC theming is removed. All sublevel rewards remain active but now carry a claim deadline: uncollected rewards expire after a configurable window.

XP & Level Progression

Define how fast players level up. In production, XP rates are configurable per channel (Sportsbook, Casino, Live, Virtual) - here we model with a single unified rate.

Curve Type:

Linear: XP scales by level number. Exponential: XP multiplies by the exponent each level. CC Redemption Rate models what % of earned CC is actually spent (players churn, hoard, or forget).

CC Economy - Sources

Where players earn Custom Currency. These are the faucets that feed CC into the economy.

Level-up CC scales: L1 = base, L2 = base × (1 + growth%), L3 = base × (1 + growth%)², etc. Sublevel CC stays flat at every level. Missions can also produce CC - not factored here.

Direct Rewards - Per Level

Besides CC, each level-up and sublevel can grant direct rewards with real EUR cost: bonus money, free spins, free bets, cashback. These bypass the CC economy entirely - they're a separate cost line.

Level-up direct rewards scale the same way as CC: base × (1 + growth%)^(level-1). Sublevel and daily login rewards stay flat. In production, each level's rewards are individually configurable from the dashboard.

XP Reduction & Benefit Lock

The platform includes configurable inactivity penalties. If a player doesn't log in or bet within a defined window, their XP is reduced by a configurable percentage - potentially causing level demotion. Benefits can also be frozen until recovery conditions are met.

Leaderboards & Progression

Leaderboards rank all players by accumulated XP over daily, weekly, and monthly periods. Directly tied to the progression system. Position-based rewards are configurable per rank tier.
